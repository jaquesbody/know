What is it? Passwords are the first line of defence. Weak or reused passwords are the cause of 80% of data breaches. A good password manager and two-factor authentication (2FA) app makes online life easier and more secure.

What about privacy? If you use an easy to guess password (e.g. Password123) or reuse a password, a single breach on one website can lead to attackers trying that same password on your bank, email, and social media. SMS-based 2FA (those 6 digit confirmation codes) is also insecure and vulnerable to SIM-swapping attacks.

We recommend Proton Pass — end-to-end encrypted, easy to use and synced between phones and laptops.

  • Download Proton Pass.
  • Add login details and new passwords as you go — don’t try to change all your passwords in one go.
  • Add the browser extension to your desktop and auto-fill passwords.
  • For greater security download Proton Authenticator and set up 2FA for critical accounts (Email, Bank, Social). You’ll need to go into Settings for each app to find 2FA.
  • Tip: Password managers often copy passwords to your clipboard. Enable “Clear clipboard after X seconds” to prevent other apps from reading it.
