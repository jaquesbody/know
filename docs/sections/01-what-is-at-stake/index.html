reclaim your digital privacy

Your digital profile isn’t just your search history. It includes your passwords, emails, contacts, location history, the metadata of your daily life (dates, times, durations and a whole lot more). This data gets sold to brokers, collected by governments, exposed in breaches and used to manipulate what you buy, believe and vote for.

Privacy and security are not the same thing. Security is about keeping people out, by using strong passwords, two-factor authentication, encryption. Privacy is about reducing what’s collected about you in the first place. You need both, but don’t confuse them.

You don’t need to become anonymous. You need to reduce your exposure. That means fewer companies collecting your data, less of your behaviour being tracked, and more control over what you share and with whom. That’s achievable. This guide shows you how.
