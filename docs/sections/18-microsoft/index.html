Know reclaim your digital privacy

Windows 10/11, Edge, Hotmail/Outlook and OneDrive all collect extensive data — including typing patterns, conversations, document and photo contents, location, file metadata and browsing habits. Microsoft also holds your BitLocker encryption keys on their servers by default, giving them (and potentially law enforcement) access without your say.

How to limit Microsoft’s reach

  • Limit data collection: Go to Settings > Privacy & Security. Turn off “Activity History”, “Diagnostic data” and “Tailored Experiences”.
  • Follow this tutorial to access BitLocker and remove Microsoft’s access to your encryption keys.
  • Reduce bloat and excess data telemetry on your Windows laptop by downloading and running privacy.sexy.
  • Move sensitive files to a local drive, disable OneDrive or use an encrypted cloud service.
  • Consider using Linux — an entirely different operating system (requires some technical know-how).
